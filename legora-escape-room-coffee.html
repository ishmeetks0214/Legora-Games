Team,

I hope this finds you well — though I am afraid this is not a social email. I am on my second espresso and it is not even 8 AM, which should tell you everything about how my morning is going. By the time this is over I will have drunk enough coffee to power a small generator.

We have a problem. A journalist from the FT called our comms team first thing this morning. They claim to have got hold of an internal document — something one of our engineers wrote raising questions about the product before we went to market. They are running a story today and have asked us to comment by close of play.

You know the product — we launched in August, it has been selling extremely well, and we have had no complaints. But the fact that this document exists, and the way it might be presented, is something I need to get ahead of before it becomes tomorrow's headline.

I have a call with our CEO and the Board Chair at 9:30 London time. That gives you about 40 minutes. I realise that is barely enough time to drink a flat white, let alone go through everything we have on file — but I need at least something on the table before my coffee gets cold. And trust me, the way this morning is going, the coffee is cooling fast.

The full set of project documents is in the review platform — probably north of 500 items at this point. Emails, contracts, meeting minutes, filings, chat logs, test reports, all sorts. Your team should have access. It is a lot to filter through, but I did not hire you for your ability to drink lattes slowly.

I have set out exactly what I need from you in the next three rooms — please go through them in order. Three rooms. Three asks. The clock is already running.

One more thing — and think of these as the extra sugar in the coffee. I know the document room better than anyone, and there are a few things buried in there that most people would walk right past. The rooms will point you at them; just keep your eyes open while you are brewing.

Right. Third espresso. The barista knows my name now. That is either a sign of loyalty or a cry for help. Please call me the moment you have something.

Thank you, team. You are not called bestteam@legorallp for nothing. When this is over, coffee is on me. And I mean the good stuff, not the instant powder from the third floor kitchen.

Best,
Sarah
